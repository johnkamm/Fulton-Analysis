Indoor Sensitivity Analysis

Pure Coast Holdings — 12504 S 42nd St, Fulton Township / Vicksburg, MI. Explore partial LED + standalone HVAC build-outs against a 3-tier financing structure.

Indoor only — outdoor expansion (Tranche 3) is not modeled here. Exploratory what-if tool, not the bank deliverable (Fulton_Bank_Proposalv2.xlsx is the live deliverable). Defaults are traceable to that workbook where noted; placeholder estimates are flagged.

What actually moves the P/L (ranked, at current defaults)

  1. Tier 3 — OpEx financing terms. The single biggest swing in this whole model, bigger than yield or price. Doubling "months of OpEx financed" from 3 → 6 moves profit by −$428K; stretching the 1-year OpEx loan term to 3–5 years moves it +$256K to +$306K. This tier's rate (20%) and 1-year term are stated placeholders (see Additional Inputs) — right now they matter more to the headline number than the actual grow economics do.
  2. Whether the improvement happens at all. Improved sq ft 0 → 10,800 swings profit by roughly −$746K. This is the core investment decision the tool exists to explore, not a hidden risk — but it's still the largest single lever.
  3. Improved-space yield rate. A ±5 g/sqft/cycle move (e.g. 40 → 45) swings profit by about ±$262K — this is the "real" production upside/downside.
  4. Sale price. A ±$50/lb move swings profit by about ±$220K — comparable in size to the yield lever, as expected since both scale revenue directly.
  5. Tier 2 — Equipment loan term. Shortening from 7 to 3 years costs about −$166K; the rate matters less (±$30–65K across a 10-point rate swing).
  6. Base OpEx line items (payroll, overhead, contingency %). A 10% change in any one is worth roughly $30K–$86K — real, but an order of magnitude smaller than the financing-tier and production levers above.
  7. Tier 1 — Mortgage rate/amortization. The smallest lever here, ±$15K–$55K across realistic ranges — it doesn't scale with the improvement decision the way the other two financing tiers do.
Bottom line: before trusting any "Profit After All Debt Service" number out of this tool, check the Tier 3 OpEx financing inputs first. They're the least grounded assumptions in the model (pure placeholders, no vendor quote or lender term sheet behind them) and currently have more influence over the headline than the production assumptions (yield, price) this tool was actually built to stress-test.

Financing Terms

Growth-rate, pricing, OpEx, and mortgage defaults are sourced from Fulton_Bank_Proposalv2.xlsx (Bank Proposal Summary, Sections 5–7). This tool replaces v2's single central 200-ton chiller with a standalone per-bay HVAC unit whose tonnage scales continuously with improved sq ft, and splits financing into 3 independently priced tiers (mortgage / equipment / OpEx) instead of v2's single property mortgage — intentional design differences, not errors, if this tool's totals don't reconcile exactly to v2 at the 10,800 sq ft / full-improvement setting.

HVAC equipment pricing is sourced from an actual Desert Aire 30-ton HVACD budgetary quote ($90K–$110K, midpoint used), scaled linearly ($/ton flat) for other tonnages. Install %, HVAC energy use (kWh/ton/yr), and all loan-term fields are still stated estimates with no source in either workbook (marked EST) — replace with real quotes/terms before using this for anything beyond directional sensitivity.
